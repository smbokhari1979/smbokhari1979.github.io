NBA Accreditation

National Board of Accreditation - Quality Assurance for Engineering Programs (Tier-1)

The National Board of Accreditation (NBA) is an autonomous body that accredits technical education programs in India. The NBA Tier-1 accreditation process evaluates programs based on Graduate Attributes and Professional Competencies Version 4.0 (GAPC V4.0). The Department of Information Technology at EASA College is committed to maintaining and enhancing quality through continuous improvement processes.

Criteria 5: Faculty Information

Marks/Weightage: 100

5.1 Student-Faculty Ratio (30 marks)

  • SFR calculated at Department level
  • Includes all UG and PG engineering programs
  • Considers allied department programs/clusters
  • Average SFR over 3 years

5.2 Faculty Qualification (25 marks)

  • Faculty Qualification Index (FQI)
  • Ph.D. and M.Tech qualified faculty
  • Required faculty calculation based on 20:1 ratio

5.3 Faculty Cadre Proportion (25 marks)

  • Ratio of Professors:Associate Professors:Assistant Professors
  • 1:2:6 proportion requirement
  • Based on AICTE/UGC norms

5.4 Visiting/Adjunct Faculty (10 marks)

  • Minimum 50 hours per year interaction
  • Industry experts, retired professors
  • Professors of practice participation

5.5 Faculty Retention (10 marks)

  • Faculty retention ratio calculation
  • Experience distribution analysis
  • Retention index over 3 years
